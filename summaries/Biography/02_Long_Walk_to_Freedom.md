<div style="margin-bottom: 60px;">
    <h3 style="color: #1a1a1a; font-size: 2rem; margin-bottom: 25px; font-weight: 700;">The Wisdom of the Cell</h3>
    <div style="column-count: 2; column-gap: 40px; text-align: justify; font-size: 1.15rem; color: #1a1a1a;">
        Mandela’s most profound transformation occurred during his two decades of incarceration. He used the solitude to study his enemies—their language (Afrikaans), their history, and their fears. He realized that the oppressor is as much a prisoner of prejudice as the oppressed is a prisoner of the state. 
        <br><br>
        His decision to embrace reconciliation upon his release was not an act of weakness, but a masterstroke of political and moral genius. By refusing to follow the path of retribution, he ensured the survival of South Africa. <em>Long Walk to Freedom</em> is a mandatory text for anyone who wants to understand the meaning of leadership, the power of forgiveness, and the absolute necessity of hope.
    </div>
