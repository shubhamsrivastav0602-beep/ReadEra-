<div style="margin-bottom: 60px; position: relative; z-index: 1;">
    <h3 style="color: #ffffff; font-size: 1.8rem; margin-bottom: 25px; border-bottom: 1px solid #333;">The Management of Complexity</h3>
    <div style="column-count: 2; column-gap: 50px; text-align: justify; font-size: 1rem; color: #dcdcdc; opacity: 0.9;">
        McConnell emphasizes that software quality is not "checked in" at the end of the project; it is "built in" during construction. He introduces the concept of "Defensive Programming"—anticipating errors and designing the system to handle them gracefully. 
        <br><br>
        He further explores the "Pragmatic" side of coding—how to write code that is not just correct, but readable and maintainable over years of evolution. <em>Code Complete</em> is a mandatory rigorous training for any professional developer. It replaces the "cowboy coding" mentality with a disciplined, high-fidelity approach to building systems that truly last. It is the roadmap for moving from a programmer to a software engineer.
