<!-- High-Level Strategy Analysis -->
<div style="margin-bottom: 60px;">
    <h3 style="color: #1a1a1a; font-size: 1.8rem; margin-bottom: 25px;">The New Groupthink</h3>
    <div style="column-count: 2; column-gap: 40px; text-align: justify; font-size: 1rem; color: #34495e;">
        Cain explores the rise of "The New Groupthink"—the belief that all creativity and productivity comes from collaboration and teamwork. She argues that this environment is often toxic for introverts, who need solitary "Deep Work" to reach their potential. 
        <br><br>
        She also delves into the biology of temperament, discussing the differences in brain chemistry (dopamine pathways) between the two types. <em>Quiet</em> is not just a book for introverts; it is a guide for anyone who manages, teaches, or lives with one. It is a call to redesign our offices, schools, and cities to allow for the quiet strength that often produces our most innovative and profound ideas.
    </div>
