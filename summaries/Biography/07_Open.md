<!-- High-Level Biographical Analysis -->
<div style="margin-bottom: 70px; position: relative; z-index: 1;">
    <h3 style="color: #1a1a1a; font-size: 2rem; margin-bottom: 25px; font-weight: 900;">The Loneliness of the Court</h3>
    <div style="column-count: 2; column-gap: 50px; text-align: justify; font-size: 1.1rem; color: #333; opacity: 0.9;">
        Agassi’s prose (assisted by J.R. Moehringer) is rhythmic and raw. He describes tennis as the most "isolated" sport—a world where you cannot talk to your team or your opponents. This isolation mirrors his internal state. His relationship with his coach **Gil Reyes** becomes the emotional anchor that allows him to finally find a reason to play: for himself, and for others. 
        <br><br>
        The book takes us through the highest highs (Wimbledon, the Olympics) and the lowest lows (dropping to world #141). It is a mandatory read, offering a masterclass in resilience and the absolute necessity of authenticity. <em>Open</em> is a testament to the fact that you can win the world and still lose yourself, and that the only true victory is finding the courage to be who you are, regardless of the score.
    </div>
