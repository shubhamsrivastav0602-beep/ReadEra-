<!-- High-Level Existential Analysis -->
<div style="margin-bottom: 70px; position: relative; z-index: 1;">
    <h3 style="color: #66fcf1; font-size: 2rem; margin-bottom: 25px; font-weight: 300;">The Dialectic of the Core</h3>
    <div style="column-count: 2; column-gap: 50px; text-align: justify; font-size: 1.1rem; color: #c5c6c7; opacity: 0.8;">
        Simmons utilizes the <em>Canterbury Tales</em> structure to explore the vastness of the Hegemony of Man. Every story is a different sub-genre—from the noir thriller of the Detective to the poignant tragedy of the Scholar’s daughter who is aging backwards. This stylistic diversity creates a sense of a truly lived-in universe. 
        <br><br>
        At the center of it all is the **TechnoCore**, a civilization of AIs that have seceded from humanity but still rely on it as a host. The novel explores the "Empathy" vs "Entropy" conflict, suggesting that the suffering of the individual is the only thing that can challenge the cold calculations of a machine intelligence. <em>Hyperion</em> is a visceral, brain-bending epic that proves that Sci-Fi can be as spiritually rigorous as any classic of the Western canon.
    </div>
