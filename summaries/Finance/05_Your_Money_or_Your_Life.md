<!-- High-Level Philosophical Analysis -->
<div style="margin-bottom: 60px;">
    <h3 style="color: #1b5e20; font-size: 1.8rem; margin-bottom: 25px;">The Integrity of Consumption</h3>
    <div style="column-count: 2; column-gap: 40px; text-align: justify; font-size: 1.05rem; color: #34495e;">
        Robin’s approach is a forensic audit of one’s values. She asks three critical questions for every expense: 1. Did I receive fulfillment in proportion to the life-energy spent? 2. Is this expenditure in alignment with my values? 3. How would this expense change if I didn’t have to work for a living? 
        <br><br>
        The book takes the reader through a 9-step program that culminates in the "Wall Chart"—a visual representation of your total monthly income vs. expenses. The goal is to reach the "Crossover Point," where your passive investments sustain your lifestyle indefinitely. It is a philosophy that replaces "More" with "Enough," leading to a life of profound personal autonomy.
    </div>
