<div style="margin-bottom: 60px; position: relative; z-index: 1;">
    <h3 style="color: #1b5e20; font-size: 1.8rem; margin-bottom: 25px;">The Paradox of Visibility</h3>
    <div style="column-count: 2; column-gap: 40px; text-align: justify; font-size: 1.05rem; color: #333;">
        The book’s primary contribution is the distinction between "Net Worth" and "Displayed Wealth." PAWs spend an average of one day per month planning their financial future, while UAWs spend their time planning their lifestyle consumption. This lead to a fundamental gap in long-term security. 
        <br><br>
        The authors also explore "Economic Outpatient Care"—the idea that parents who subsidize their adult children’s lifestyles often destroy their ability to accumulate wealth. <em>The Millionaire Next Door</em> is a visceral, reality-check for any high-earner who isn't actually becoming wealthy. It is a philosophy that replaces the "keeping up with the Joneses" mentality with a rigorous focus on financial independence and personal responsibility.
