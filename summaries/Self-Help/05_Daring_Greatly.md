<div style="margin-bottom: 60px;">
    <h3 style="color: #4a4a4a; font-size: 1.8rem; margin-bottom: 25px;">The Shielding of the Self</h3>
    <div style="column-count: 2; column-gap: 40px; text-align: justify; font-size: 1rem; color: #5a5a5a;">
        Brown identifies "Shame" as the primary barrier to vulnerability. It is the gremlin that whispers "You're not good enough." To counter this, she proposes **Shame Resilience**—the ability to acknowledge shame, talk about it, and move through it with self-compassion. 
        <br><br>
        She also warns against the "Armor" we wear: perfectionism, numbing (through work, food, or screens), and "Foreboding Joy" (the fear that if things are good, something bad must happen). <em>Daring Greatly</em> calls for **Wholehearted Living**, which means engaging in our lives from a place of worthiness rather than a place of lack. It is a profound psychological guide for anyone seeking deeper connection and authentic leadership.
    </div>
