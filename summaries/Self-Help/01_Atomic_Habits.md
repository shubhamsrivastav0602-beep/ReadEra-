<div style="margin-bottom: 50px;">
    <h3 style="color: #f39c12; font-size: 1.5rem;">The Identity Paradox</h3>
    <div style="column-count: 2; column-gap: 40px; text-align: justify; font-size: 1rem; color: #34495e;">
        Clear’s most profound insight is the "Identity-Based Habit." Traditional self-help asks you to focus on outcomes—losing 10kg or writing a book. Clear argues that sustainable change only occurs when you shift your internal narrative. You aren't someone who is trying to run; you <em>are</em> a runner. Every action you take is a "vote" for the person you want to become. By winning these micro-elections daily, you slowly but surely rebuild your sense of self.
        <br><br>
        The book further explores the "Plateau of Latent Potential." Often, we give up because the 1% gains aren't immediately visible—like an ice cube that doesn't melt at 31 degrees but finally does at 32. All the energy was being stored, not wasted. This perspective provides the emotional stamina required for true mastery.
