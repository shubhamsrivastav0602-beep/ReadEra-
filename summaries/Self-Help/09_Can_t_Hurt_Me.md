    <h3 style="color: #fff; font-size: 2rem; margin-bottom: 25px; font-family: 'Impact'; text-transform: uppercase;">Callousing the Mind</h3>
    <div style="column-count: 2; column-gap: 50px; text-align: justify; font-size: 1.05rem; color: #bbb; font-family: 'Verdana';">
        Goggins’ most powerful concept is the "Calloused Mind." He argues that just as hands get calloused through hard labor, the mind must be calloused through suffering. By deliberately seeking out friction and discomfort, you build a mental layer that is impervious to pain and doubt. 
        <br><br>
        He introduces the "Accountability Mirror"—the practice of staring at yourself and honestly acknowledging your weaknesses. There is no room for excuses in Goggins’ world. He also explores "Taking Souls"—the act of outworking your competition so decisively that they give up before the race even begins. <em>Can't Hurt Me</em> is a visceral, high-intensity rigorous training that proves that human potential is limited only by our willingness to endure the unendurable. It is a mandatory read for anyone who wants to stop being a victim and start being a warrior.
