<!-- High-Level Stoic Analysis -->
<div style="margin-bottom: 70px; position: relative; z-index: 1;">
    <h3 style="color: #1a1a1a; font-size: 2rem; margin-bottom: 30px; font-weight: 200; font-style: italic;">The Inner Citadel</h3>
    <div style="column-count: 2; column-gap: 50px; text-align: justify; font-size: 1.1rem; color: #1a1a1a; opacity: 0.9;">
        Aurelius emphasizes the concept of the "Inner Citadel"—a mental fortress that can remains calm and virtuous regardless of the external storm. He argues that things do not touch the soul, but stand discreetly outside it. The only true "good" is virtue (justice, temperance, wisdom, and courage), and the only true "bad" is vice. 
        <br><br>
        His prose is repetitive, disciplined, and brutal in its honesty. He constantly reminds himself of his own mortality (<em>Memento Mori</em>) and the fleeting nature of fame. <em>Meditations</em> is a mandatory rigorous training for any leader or seeker, providing a visceral and high-fidelity roadmap for building an unbreakable character. It is a work of absolute intellectual integrity that has guided thinkers for nearly two millennia.
    </div>
