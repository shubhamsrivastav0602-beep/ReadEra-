    <h3 style="color: #27ae60; margin-top: 0;">📝 FULL SUMMARY</h3>
    <p>Robert Kiyosaki’s <em>Rich Dad Poor Dad</em> is a paradigm-shifting guide to wealth. It is built around the lessons Kiyosaki learned from two father figures: his biological father (Poor Dad), who was highly educated but struggled financially, and his best friend's father (Rich Dad), a high-school dropout who became one of Hawaii's wealthiest men. The book breaks down the different mentalities regarding money, explaining that the traditional advice—"Go to school, get a good job, and save money"—is becoming obsolete in the modern economy.</p>
    
    <p>The core of the book is defined by the "Cash Flow Quadrant." Rich people focus on the B (Business) and I (Investor) quadrants, while the poor and middle class stay in the E (Employee) and S (Self-Employed) quadrants. Kiyosaki emphasizes that the most powerful asset we have is our mind. By investing in financial education, people can break free from the "Rat Race" of living paycheck to paycheck and build a legacy of sustainable wealth.</p>
