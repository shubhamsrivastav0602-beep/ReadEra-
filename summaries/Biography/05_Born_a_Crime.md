    <h3 style="color: #1a1a1a; font-size: 2rem; margin-bottom: 25px; font-weight: 900; text-transform: uppercase;">The Language of the Oppressor</h3>
    <div style="column-count: 2; column-gap: 50px; text-align: justify; font-size: 1.1rem; color: #1a1a1a;">
        Noah’s most profound insight is how language can bypass racial barriers. By learning to speak multiple tribal languages and English perfectly, he became a "chameleon" who could navigate the dangerous social landscape of Apartheid. 
        <br><br>
        The heart of the book, however, is his mother, Patricia. A fiercely religious and independent woman, she raised Trevor to be a "human being" in a country that wanted him to be a "statistic." From being thrown out of a moving car to survive a hitman to surviving a near-fatal shooting, her resilience is the engine of this story. <em>Born a Crime</em> is a visceral reminder that even in the darkest, most absurd circumstances, humor and love are the ultimate acts of rebellion.
