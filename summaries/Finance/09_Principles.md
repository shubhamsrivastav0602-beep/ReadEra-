    <div style="column-count: 2; column-gap: 50px; text-align: justify; font-size: 1rem; color: #333333; opacity: 0.9;">
        Dalio proposes that successful living is a matter of "hyper-realism." He emphasizes the importance of knowing what you don't know and actively seeking out the smartest people who disagree with you. This lead to his "Idea Meritocracy"—a system where the best ideas win regardless of hierarchy. 
        <br><br>
        He also outlines the "5-Step Process" to get what you want out of life: Go for goals, identify and don't tolerate problems, diagnose problems to get at their root causes, design plans that will get you around them, and do what's necessary to push through these designs to results. <em>Principles</em> is a mandatory read, offering a cold, precise, and high-fidelity roadmap for anyone who wants to optimize their existence with the same rigor Dalio used to conquer Wall Street.
