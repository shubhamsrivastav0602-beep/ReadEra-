<div style="margin-bottom: 70px; position: relative; z-index: 1;">
    <h3 style="color: #1a2e35; font-size: 2rem; margin-bottom: 30px; font-weight: 200; font-style: italic;">The Singularity of Being</h3>
    <div style="column-count: 2; column-gap: 50px; text-align: justify; font-size: 1.1rem; color: #1a2e35; opacity: 0.9;">
        Hesse’s genius lies in his synthesis of Western individualism and Eastern spirituality. Siddhartha’s path is not one of denial, but of total immersion. He must become a merchant, a lover, and a father before he can become a sage. This suggests that the "sin" and the "worldliness" are not obstacles to enlightenment, but the very materials of it. 
        <br><br>
        The final revelation with Vasudeva, the ferryman, is one of the most sublime moments in literature. By listening to the many voices of the river, Siddhartha realizes that all things are interconnected—the child and the man, the saint and the sinner. <em>Siddhartha</em> is a mandatory rigorous training for any seeker, providing a serene and high-fidelity roadmap for the evolution of the consciousness. It is a work of absolute harmony.
