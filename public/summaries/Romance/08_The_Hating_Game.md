## 💕 LOVE STORY ARC: Rivals to Lovers

### THE MEET-CUTE: "You look like a librarian"

Lucy Hutton and Joshua Templeman work at the same publishing company—they sit across from each other, compete for the same promotions, and have been enemies since the day they met. They hate everything about each other: his smug smile, her cheerful optimism, his perfection, her messiness.

The hate is real—but it's also something else. Something that simmers beneath the surface, something that makes every interaction feel like a match of wits.

When a promotion becomes available—manager of both their departments—they both want it. And the competition gets intense.

### THE CONFLICT: The Promotion and the Attraction

The more they compete, the more they notice each other. Lucy sees Joshua's vulnerability beneath his armor. Joshua sees Lucy's strength beneath her klutziness. They start playing games—not just the rivalry games, but other games. Challenges. Bets.

And somewhere along the way, the hate becomes something else.

But they can't be together—they're rivals. If they get together, someone will have to leave. And neither of them wants to lose.

### THE GRAND GESTURE: The Dinner

Joshua invites Lucy to dinner—not as a date, but as a chance to finally be honest. They both admit what's been growing between them. And they realize that the only way to win is to stop playing games.

### THE HEA: Together

In the end, neither of them gets the promotion. But they get something better—each other. And they realize that winning isn't about the job, it's about finding someone who makes you whole.

Lucy is cheerful, optimistic, and deeply competitive. She hides her insecurity behind humor and her fear of failure behind her bubbly personality. She has always been the "nice girl"—the one who doesn't compete, doesn't fight back. But with Joshua, she's finally found someone who challenges her.
