<!-- High-Level Existential Analysis -->
<div style="margin-bottom: 70px; position: relative; z-index: 1;">
    <h3 style="color: #1a1a1a; font-size: 2rem; margin-bottom: 30px; font-weight: 200; font-style: italic;">The Invisibility of the Soul</h3>
    <div style="column-count: 2; column-gap: 50px; text-align: justify; font-size: 1.15rem; color: #444; opacity: 0.9;">
        Saint-Exupéry’s brilliance lies in his ability to articulate the "spirituality" of childhood. He argues that as we grow up, we lose the ability to see the world as it truly is—a place of wonder and connection. Instead, we see it as a collection of "matters of consequence" (business, accounting, rules). 
        <br><br>
        The relationship between the Prince and his Rose is a metaphor for the complexity of love and the pain of departure. The Fox’s secret—that the essential is invisible—serves as the novel’s moral anchor. <em>The Little Prince</em> is a mandatory read, offering a lyrical and high-fidelity roadmap for reclaiming the innocence of the heart. It is a work of absolute radiance that reminds us that we were all children once, even if few of us remember it.
    </div>
