<div style="margin-bottom: 60px;">
    <h3 style="color: #1b5e20; font-size: 1.8rem; margin-bottom: 25px;">The Paradox of Mr. Market</h3>
    <div style="column-count: 2; column-gap: 40px; text-align: justify; font-size: 1.05rem; color: #333;">
        Graham’s most enduring metaphor is "Mr. Market"—a manic-depressive partner who offers to buy or sell his interest to you every day. Sometimes his prices are ridiculous, sometimes they are sober. The intelligent investor's job is not to follow Mr. Market, but to take advantage of his mood swings. 
        <br><br>
        The most critical concept is the <strong>Margin of Safety</strong>. Investing without a margin of safety is like building a bridge that can only hold a 10-ton truck, then driving a 10-ton truck over it. You must buy at a price that allows for the inevitability of human error and economic downturns. <em>The Intelligent Investor</em> is the ultimate guide to keeping your head when everyone else is losing theirs.
