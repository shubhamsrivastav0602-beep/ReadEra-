<div style="margin-bottom: 60px;">
    <h3 style="color: #1a1a1a; font-size: 1.8rem; margin-bottom: 25px;">The Chemistry of the End</h3>
    <div style="column-count: 2; column-gap: 40px; text-align: justify; font-size: 1rem; color: #2d3436;">
        Kolbert focuses on the "Invisibles"—the ocean acidification and the warming of the atmosphere—that are the primary drivers of this event. She explains that species cannot evolve fast enough to cope with the rapid changes we’ve introduced. From the gold frogs of Panama to the Corals of Queensland, the narrative is a eulogy for a world we are losing in real-time. 
        <br><br>
        The book is a masterpiece of scientific journalism. It avoids hyperbole, relying instead on the overwhelming weight of evidence from the field. <em>The Sixth Extinction</em> is a mandatory rigorous training for anyone who wants to understand the true scale of the ecological crisis and the absolute necessity of a shift in how we perceive our place in the web of life. It is a sobering, beautiful, and profoundly important work.
