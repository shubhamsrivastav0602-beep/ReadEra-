<div style="margin-bottom: 60px; position: relative; z-index: 1;">
    <h3 style="color: #1a1a1a; font-size: 1.8rem; margin-bottom: 25px;">The Corruption of Hope</h3>
    <div style="column-count: 2; column-gap: 40px; text-align: justify; font-size: 1.05rem; color: #333;">
        Gatsby’s tragedy is not that he fails to win Daisy, but that he fails to see that Daisy is unworthy of his epic "Platonic conception" of her. Fitzgerald uses the setting of West Egg vs. East Egg to dissect the rigid class structures of America. While the "New Money" (Gatsby) is garish but hopeful, the "Old Money" (The Buchanans) is refined but fundamentally hollow and careless. 
        <br><br>
        The novel is saturated with themes of time and waste. The "Valley of Ashes"—a grey wasteland between the city and the shore—stands as a grim reminder of the human cost of the Dream. The final, haunting paragraph of the novel is arguably the greatest in American literature, capturing the universal human struggle to move forward while being tethered to our own myths.
    </div>
