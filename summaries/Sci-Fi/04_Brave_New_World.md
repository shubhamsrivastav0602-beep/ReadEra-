<div style="margin-bottom: 60px;">
    <h3 style="color: #2980b9; font-size: 1.8rem; margin-bottom: 25px;">The Savage and the Civilization</h3>
    <div style="column-count: 2; column-gap: 40px; text-align: justify; font-size: 1rem; color: #444;">
        The tragedy of <em>Brave New World</em> unfolds when John "The Savage"—born in a reservation outside the World State's reach—is brought into the "civilized" world. John, fueled by the works of Shakespeare and the reality of suffering, becomes a disruptive force. He cannot accept a world without God, poetry, or pain. 
        <br><br>
        His famous debate with Mustapha Mond, the World Controller, is the philosophical heart of the book. Mond argues that universal happiness is worth the price of art and science. John, however, claims "the right to be unhappy." Huxley forces us to ask: If we are all 100% happy but have 0% soul, have we actually won?
    </div>
