<div style="margin-bottom: 70px; position: relative; z-index: 1;">
    <h3 style="color: #1a1a1a; font-size: 1.8rem; margin-bottom: 25px; font-weight: 400; font-style: italic;">The Courage to Swim to Shore</h3>
    <div style="column-count: 2; column-gap: 50px; text-align: justify; font-size: 1.05rem; color: #444; opacity: 0.9;">
        Hoover masterfully illustrates how abuse is not always black and white. Ryle is not a "villain" in the traditional sense; he is a multifaceted man haunted by his own trauma. This makes Lily’s struggle all the more agonizing—how do you choose between the person you love and your own survival? 
        <br><br>
        The novel’s power lies in Lily’s refusal to be a victim. By identifying the cycle of abuse that her mother endured, she realizes that the only way to protect her own daughter is to break that cycle herself. Atlas serves as the "what if" that becomes a reality of hope. <em>It Ends with Us</em> is a mandatory read, providing a window into the silent strength required to say "no" to a cycle that has lasted for generations. It is an anthem of self-love and resilience.
