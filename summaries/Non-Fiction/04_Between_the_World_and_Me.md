<!-- Philosophical Breakdown -->
<div style="margin-bottom: 60px;">
    <h3 style="color: #000; font-size: 1.8rem; margin-bottom: 25px; border-bottom: 1px solid #eee; padding-bottom: 10px;">The Anatomy of the Dream</h3>
    <div style="column-count: 2; column-gap: 40px; text-align: justify; font-size: 1.1rem; color: #1a1a1a;">
        Coates uses history as a forensic tool. He dissects the notion of "race" as a social construct designed to justify the preservation of power. His prose is melodic but unflinching, drawing from the works of James Baldwin to create a narrative that is both personal and prophetic. 
        <br><br>
        He warns his son that there is no easy hope—only the struggle. The "Dreamers" are those who live in the delusion of an innocent history. For Coates, the only path to sanity is a clear-eyed recognition of the physical world and the conscious refusal to let the Dreamers define your reality or your worth.
    </div>
