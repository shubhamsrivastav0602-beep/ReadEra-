    <h3 style="color: #111; font-size: 2rem; margin-bottom: 30px; font-weight: 200; font-style: italic;">The Egoic Hall of Mirrors</h3>
    <div style="column-count: 2; column-gap: 50px; text-align: justify; font-size: 1.1rem; color: #333; opacity: 0.9;">
        Tolle’s most profound insight is the "Pain-Body"—an accumulation of old emotional pain that lives in every human being. He explains how the ego feeds on this pain to maintain its false sense of self. By practicing "presence," we effectively starve the ego and the pain-body of their fuel (our attention). 
        <br><br>
        The book handles complex metaphysical concepts with a rare clarity. It moves beyond "motivation" into the realm of radical self-observation. Tolle argues that enlightenment is not a "future achievement" but a "present realization." <em>The Power of Now</em> is a mandatory rigorous training for anyone seeking to transcend the noise of the modern world and rediscover the underlying stillness of their own being. It is a lighthouse in a sea of mental chaos.
