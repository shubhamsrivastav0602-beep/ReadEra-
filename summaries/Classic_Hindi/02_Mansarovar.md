<!-- High-Level Literary Analysis -->
<div style="margin-bottom: 70px; position: relative; z-index: 1;">
    <h3 style="color: #1a1a1a; font-size: 2rem; margin-bottom: 30px; font-weight: 200; font-style: italic;">The Anatomy of the Human Soul</h3>
    <div style="column-count: 2; column-gap: 50px; text-align: justify; font-size: 1.15rem; color: #333; opacity: 0.9;">
        Premchand’s short stories are masterful studies in psychology and social critique. He doesn't just tell a story; he provokes a conscience. In <em>Idgah</em>, he uses a child’s selflessness to critique adult greed; in <em>Panch Parmeshwar</em>, he explores the divinity of justice. His prose is simple yet profound, often ending in a moral or psychological epiphany. 
        <br><br>
        The collection serves as a "visceral" history of early 20th-century India. It explores the dark corners of the caste system, the plight of widows, and the erosion of rural values. <em>Mansarovar</em> is a mandatory read, offering a high-fidelity roadmap of the Indian heart. It is a work of absolute authenticity that remain the benchmark for short-story writing in any language.
