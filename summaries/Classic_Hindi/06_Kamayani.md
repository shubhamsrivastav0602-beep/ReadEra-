<div style="margin-bottom: 70px; position: relative; z-index: 1;">
    <h3 style="color: #3e2723; font-size: 2rem; margin-bottom: 30px; font-weight: 200; font-style: italic;">The Philosophy of Samrasta</h3>
    <div style="column-count: 2; column-gap: 50px; text-align: justify; font-size: 1.15rem; color: #5d4037; opacity: 0.9;">
        Prasad’s brilliance lies in his "Chhayavadi" imagery—a mystical, romantic style that elevated Hindi poetry to global standards. Through the characters of **Manu** (Mind), **Shraddha** (Heart), and **Ida** (Intellect), he explores the eternal conflict between emotion and reason. The final goal is **Samrasta**—a state of equanimity where the individual merges with the cosmic.
        <br><br>
        The poem tracks the failure of Ida's materialistic, intellect-driven city (Saraswat Pradesh) and advocates for Shraddha's path of love and sacrifice. <em>Kamayani</em> is a mandatory read, offering a lyrical and high-fidelity roadmap for the evolution of the human spirit. It is a work of absolute historical and philosophical integrity that stands as the crown jewel of Hindi literature.
