<div style="margin-bottom: 70px; position: relative; z-index: 1;">
    <h3 style="color: #3e2723; font-size: 2rem; margin-bottom: 30px; font-weight: 200; font-style: italic;">The Unified Theory of Everything</h3>
    <div style="column-count: 2; column-gap: 50px; text-align: justify; font-size: 1.1rem; color: #5d4037; opacity: 0.9;">
        Isaacson’s most profound insight is Leonardo’s "cross-disciplinary" mind. He didn't see art and science as separate, but as different ways to understand the patterns of nature. His anatomical dissections informed the musculature of his paintings; his studies of water flow led to his revolutionary landscape techniques. 
        <br><br>
        The book explores his famous "fantasies"—the flying machines and military tanks that were centuries ahead of their time. It also handles his personal life—his homosexuality, his procrastination, and his intense sense of wonder—with immense empathy. <em>Leonardo da Vinci</em> is a mandatory read, offering an intellectual and high-fidelity roadmap for anyone who wants to rediscover the beauty of curiosity. It is a work of absolute radiance that shows how one man attempted to capture the soul of the universe.
