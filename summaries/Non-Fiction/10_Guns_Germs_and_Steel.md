<!-- High-Level Analytical Analysis -->
<div style="margin-bottom: 60px; position: relative; z-index: 1;">
    <h3 style="color: #3e2723; font-size: 1.8rem; margin-bottom: 25px;">The Axis of Fate</h3>
    <div style="column-count: 2; column-gap: 40px; text-align: justify; font-size: 1.05rem; color: #5d4037;">
        Diamond’s most compelling argument is the "East-West Axis" of Eurasia. Because plants and animals can spread more easily along lines of latitude (similar climate) than longitude (varying climate), Eurasia developed agriculture and civilization at a much faster rate than the Americas or Africa. This lead to a surplus of food, allowing for the development of specialists—inventors, soldiers, and kings. 
        <br><br>
        He further explores the dark side of this development: the co-evolution of humans and domestic animals led to the creation of cross-species "Germs" that decimated populations in the New World who had no immunity. <em>Guns, Germs, and Steel</em> is a masterpiece of interdisciplinary research, blending biology, linguistics, and archaeology to provide a unified theory of human inequality. It remains a mandatory rigorous training for anyone seeking to understand the deep, structural roots of the modern world.
