<div style="margin-bottom: 70px; position: relative; z-index: 1;">
    <h3 style="color: #1a1a1a; font-size: 2rem; margin-bottom: 25px; font-weight: 300;">The Art of the Deep</h3>
    <div style="column-count: 2; column-gap: 50px; text-align: justify; font-size: 1.1rem; color: #333333; opacity: 0.9;">
        Newport proposes four "Rules" for achieving a state of depth: Work Deeply, Embrace Boredom, Quit Social Media, and Drain the Shallows. He explains how our current obsession with "real-time communication" (Slack, email, etc.) is creating a state of "attention residue," where the mind is never fully present in a single task. 
        <br><br>
        He further explores the neuro-science of concentration—how myelination of neural pathways occurs during intense focus, effectively "hard-wiring" the brain for mastery. <em>Deep Work</em> is a mandatory rigorous training for any modern knowledge worker. It is the definitive guide to reclaiming your cognitive agency and producing work that truly matters. It is a philosophy that replaces the "busyness" of the modern office with the intense, satisfying silence of the deep mind.
