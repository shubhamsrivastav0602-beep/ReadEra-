<div style="margin-bottom: 70px; position: relative; z-index: 1;">
    <h3 style="color: #111; font-size: 1.8rem; margin-bottom: 30px; letter-spacing: 2px;">The Paradox of the Empty Bowl</h3>
    <div style="column-count: 2; column-gap: 50px; text-align: justify; font-size: 1.1rem; color: #444;">
        Lao Tzu argues that the usefulness of a bowl comes from its emptiness, not its solid parts. This metaphor extends to the human soul: when we empty ourselves of ego, desire, and the need for recognition, we become useful instruments of the Tao. 
        <br><br>
        He also introduces the concept of "The Great Integrity"—the idea that a leader who governs best is the one whose subjects barely know he exists. It is a philosophy of subtraction rather than addition. In a world obsessed with 'More,' the <em>Tao Te Ching</em> is a radical call for 'Enough.' It is a timeless invitation to return to our original, uncarved nature.
    </div>
