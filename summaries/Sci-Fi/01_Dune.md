<!-- Narrative Epic Prose -->
<div style="margin-bottom: 60px;">
    <h3 style="color: #ffa500; font-size: 1.5rem;">The Transfiguration of Paul Atreides</h3>
    <div style="column-count: 2; column-gap: 40px; text-align: justify; font-size: 1rem; color: #00ffccdd;">
        The narrative arc of Paul Atreides is not a traditional hero's journey, but a subversion of it. Herbert presents Paul's rise to power as a terrifying inevitability. Gifted with the prescient ability to see future "threads," Paul becomes trapped by his own visions. His struggle is not just against the Harkonnens, but against the very prophecy that names him the Kwisatz Haderach.
        <br><br>
        On Arrakis, where every drop of water is tallied and every breath is a survival calculation, the religious mythos of the Fremen becomes Paul’s greatest weapon and his heaviest burden. <em>Dune</em> asks a haunting question: Can humanity survive its own gods? The resolution is a planetary-scale coup that changes the course of galactic history forever.
    </div>
