<div style="margin-bottom: 70px; position: relative; z-index: 1;">
    <h3 style="color: #1a1a1a; font-size: 2rem; margin-bottom: 30px; font-weight: 200; font-style: italic;">The Curse of Solitude</h3>
    <div style="column-count: 2; column-gap: 50px; text-align: justify; font-size: 1.15rem; color: #1a1a1a; opacity: 0.9;">
        Márquez’s prose is lush, rhythmic, and infinitely evocative. He explores the concept of "Solitude"—not as loneliness, but as the fundamental human condition. Each member of the Buendía family is trapped in their own obsession, unable to truly connect with others or escape the circularity of their fate. 
        <br><br>
        The town of Macondo itself serves as a metaphor for the history of Latin America—from its idealistic founding to its exploitation and ultimate annihilation. The novel is a visceral, high-fidelity experience that demands the reader surrender to its internal logic. <em>One Hundred Years of Solitude</em> is a mandatory read, offering a roadmap of the soul's most intricate and mysterious geography. It is a work of absolute genius.
    </div>
