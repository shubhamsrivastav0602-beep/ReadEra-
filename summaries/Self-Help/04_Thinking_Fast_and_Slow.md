<!-- High-Level Cognitive Analysis -->
<div style="margin-bottom: 60px;">
    <h3 style="color: #1a1a1a; font-size: 1.8rem; margin-bottom: 25px; border-bottom: 1px solid #eee;">The Illusion of Validity</h3>
    <div style="column-count: 2; column-gap: 40px; text-align: justify; font-size: 1.05rem; color: #34495e;">
        Kahneman explores the "Law of Small Numbers" and the "Availability Heuristic"—our tendency to overestimate the probability of events that are easy to remember. He reveals that even experts are susceptible to "Overconfidence Bias," often predicting the future with conviction while ignoring the role of luck. 
        <br><br>
        His discussion on **Prospect Theory** is revolutionary, showing that humans are "Loss Averse"—we feel the pain of a loss twice as intensely as the joy of a gain. This insight has transformed economics, finance, and public policy. The book is a mandatory rigorous training for anyone who wants to think more clearly and avoid the common pitfalls of the human psyche.
    </div>
