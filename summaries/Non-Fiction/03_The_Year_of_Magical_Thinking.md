<div style="margin-bottom: 60px;">
    <h3 style="color: #1a1a1a; font-size: 1.8rem; margin-bottom: 25px;">The Grammar of Mourning</h3>
    <div style="column-count: 2; column-gap: 40px; text-align: justify; font-size: 1.05rem; color: #34495e;">
        "Magical Thinking" is the belief that one's thoughts or actions can reverse the irreversible. Didion chronicles how she refused to throw away her husband’s shoes, believing he would need them when he came back. This isnt sentimentality; it is a profound psychological defense mechanism. 
        <br><br>
        Didion’s writing remains detached, almost reports-like, which makes the underlying pain even more resonant. She examines why we fear the "nothingness" of death and how we use language to buffer ourselves against the sheer unpredictability of life. It is a book that doesn't offer easy comfort, only the stark truth of what it means to be human and to lose.
    </div>
