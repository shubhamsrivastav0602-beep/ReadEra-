<div style="margin-bottom: 70px; position: relative; z-index: 1;">
    <h3 style="color: #1a1a1a; font-size: 2.2rem; margin-bottom: 30px; font-weight: 200; font-style: italic;">The Anatomy of Partition</h3>
    <div style="column-count: 2; column-gap: 50px; text-align: justify; font-size: 1.15rem; color: #1a1a1a; opacity: 0.9;">
        Kamleshwar’s prose is grand, somber, and deeply intellectual. He moves through eras and continents, showing how the same "Pakistan" (symbolizing partition and hate) has been built in various forms throughout history—from the Crusades to the World Wars and the sub-continent's partition. The novel is a visceral interrogation of the human soul's capacity for cruelty and its desperate need for redemption. 
        <br><br>
        The figure of **The Adeeb** (The Writer) who presides over the court serves as the collective conscience of humanity. <em>Kitne Pakistan</em> is a mandatory read, offering a heartbreaking and high-fidelity roadmap of our shared history. It is a work of absolute intellectual bravery that remain the definitive statement on the tragedy of human division.
