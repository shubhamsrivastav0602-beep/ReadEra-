    <h3 style="color: #ffa500; font-size: 1.8rem; margin-bottom: 25px;">The Mastery of Seldon Crises</h3>
    <div style="column-count: 2; column-gap: 40px; text-align: justify; font-size: 1.05rem; color: #00ffccdd;">
        Asimov’s narrative structure is unique: it jumps across centuries, focusing on "Seldon Crises"—moments where the Foundation is forced to solve a problem with only one logical outcome. Through trade, religion, and diplomacy, the tiny planet Terminus gradually becomes the dominant force in the galaxy. 
        <br><br>
        The novel is a meditation on the power of knowledge versus the fragility of power. While the Empire has the warships, the Foundation has the science. It challenges the reader to look at history not as a series of accidents, but as a grand equation that, if understood, can be optimized for the survival of the species.
