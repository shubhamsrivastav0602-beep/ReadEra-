<!-- High-Level Sociological Analysis -->
<div style="margin-bottom: 70px; position: relative; z-index: 1;">
    <h3 style="color: #1a1a1a; font-size: 2rem; margin-bottom: 25px; font-weight: 900; text-transform: uppercase;">The Beautiful Forever</h3>
    <div style="column-count: 2; column-gap: 50px; text-align: justify; font-size: 1.1rem; color: #1a1a1a;">
        The title comes from a wall near the slum that advertises Italian floor tiles with the slogan "Beautiful Forever." This wall hide the reality of Annawadi from the tourists. Boo focuses on **Abdul**, a dedicated trash sorter, and **Asha**, a woman trying to leverage corruption to become a political master. Through their stories, She exposes the "globalized" nature of poverty, where even the garbage of the rich is a contested resource. 
        <br><br>
        The book is visceral, often painful, but never condescending. It reveals how the crushing weight of systemic failure and police brutality forces individuals to make impossible moral choices. Yet, amidst the grime, there are flashes of intense humanity and hope. <em>Behind the Beautiful Forevers</em> is an essential archive of late-stage capitalism and the resilience of the human spirit in the most precarious of circumstances.
